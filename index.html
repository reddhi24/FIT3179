World Heritage Sites

-A visualisation by Reddhi Poddar

World Heritage Sites are the areas or landmarks around the world protected for their cultural, scientific, historical or other forms of significance. The international treaty called the Convention concerning the Protection of the World Cultural and Natural Heritage, adopted by the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) on 16 November 1972, strives to encourage the identification, protection and preservation of cultural and natural heritage around the world. Till date there are 1,121 sites across 167 countries after 29 new sites were added in the year 2019.

UNESCO inscribes a site as a world heritage site if it meets at least on the ten criteria set by it and is of "outstanding universal value". There are six criteria for cultural sites and four for natural sites. These criteria were two different sets until 2004. However on 2005, they were modified into a single set of criteria. These criteria can be found here.

A site may be added to the list of World Heritage in Danger if conditions threaten the characteristics for which the landmark or area was inscribed on the World Heritage List. These problems may involve armed conflict and war, natural disasters, pollution, poaching, or uncontrolled urbanisation or human development. This danger list is intended to increase international awareness of the threats and to encourage counteractive measures.This list is reviewed annually and sites are added or removed as they see fit.
